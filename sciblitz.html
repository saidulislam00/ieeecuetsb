About SCIBLITZ

SCIBLITZ 1.0 is a student-led model conference crafted by IEEE CUET Student Branch. It blends academic rigour with engaging communication, empowering undergraduates and young researchers to present high-impact ideas within a concise and professional format.

Designed as a 3 Minute Thesis‚Äìstyle competition, SCIBLITZ 1.0 focuses on how clearly and confidently participants can communicate their research. Competition Tracks

Choose the track that fits your work ‚Äî or challenge yourself and participate in both. Each track has its own judging criteria, formats and prize categories.

Track 01

3 Minute Thesis (3MT)

Summarise your entire research project in a 3-minute, single-slide presentation. Convince the judges that your problem matters, your method is solid, and your results are impactful ‚Äî without drowning the audience in equations.
  • Team of up to 6 members.
  • Phase 1: Abstract submission & online screening.
  • Phase 2: On-site 3 minute presentations with Q&A.
  • Judged on clarity, structure, visuals, innovation & delivery.
Elevator Pitch Skill Conference Simulation Judge & Audience Score

Track 02

Poster Presentation

Translate your research into a visually appealing scientific poster. Tell the story of your objectives, methodology, findings and applications using graphics, diagrams and compelling design.
  • Team of up to 3 members.
  • Phase 1: Draft poster submission for screening.
  • Phase 2: On-site poster display & evaluation.
  • Judged on content depth, design, originality & explanation.
Technical Posters Graphical Storytelling Interactive Discussion

Who Can Participate?

SCIBLITZ is open to students from all engineering & science backgrounds. Teams can submit research works, thesis projects, competition entries or self-initiated innovations.

Undergraduate Students
  • Students from any engineering or science department.
  • Solo or team-based participation allowed.
  • Thesis or course project works are eligible.
Independent Researchers
  • Students working on personal or competition projects.
  • Early-stage ideas or partial results accepted.
  • Encouraged to submit posters or early abstracts.
Research-Oriented Clubs
  • University clubs may submit group research works.
  • Mixed-year teams allowed.
  • Cross-university collaboration permitted.
